Pricing questions, answered

How billing works, what these placeholder numbers mean, and how to keep costs under control

Everything is pay-as-you-use and billed by the hour. The monthly figure shown is simply the hourly rate × 730 (the average hours in a month) so you can compare at a glance — you are still billed per hour for on-demand resources.

No. Every figure on this page is an indicative placeholder in Toman, derived from our internal unit-rate model at a placeholder rate of 1 USD = 60,000 Toman. They keep the whole ladder consistent, but they are not committed prices — final pricing is confirmed at launch.

Only the view. Monthly = hourly × 730. Toggle it to compare a quick monthly estimate against the hourly rate you are actually charged.

On-demand resources bill only while they run (the Observed mode). For steady workloads you can reserve capacity (Reserved-max) or mix both (Hybrid) on Kubernetes and HPC clusters.

Power off or delete the resource. Servers and clusters bill while they are active; deleting a cluster stops its charges immediately.

Inbound and in-network traffic are free. Outbound egress has a fair-use quota; beyond it, transfer is billed per GB at the placeholder network rate.
