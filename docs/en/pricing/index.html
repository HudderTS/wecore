Deployment models

Three ways to run WECORE.

The same platform, in the model that matches your organization's goal. The foundation is identical — only where it runs and who owns it changes.
Current focus

On-Premise deployment

For when maximum data sovereignty comes first. WECORE deploys on infrastructure you own or lease; your data and control plane stay with you.

  • Single-tenant, on your own hardware
  • Full data sovereignty, no data egress
  • Fits enterprises, industry, and air-gapped environments
Current focus

Join WECORE Hub

For those who want to turn infrastructure into revenue. Join your capacity to the Hub and make it a sellable, billable product.

  • Turns raw capacity into a sellable product
  • Catalog, quota, and billing ready out of the box
  • Fits universities and datacenters

Hybrid

Keep your own infrastructure and burst to Hub capacity at peak — one platform, two sources of capacity.

  • Burst to Hub under peak load
  • The same workflow on both sides
  • Balance ownership with elasticity

A self-service, hourly public cloud is on the roadmap and is not offered in the current phase.

FAQ

Pricing questions, answered

How pricing is set, what affects it, and how to get a tailored quote

Because WECORE is not a retail public cloud. Every deployment is estimated from its model (On-Premise or WECORE Hub), the size of the infrastructure, GPU, and the level of support — so pricing comes as a tailored quote, not a one-size-fits-all tariff.

On-Premise deploys on infrastructure you own or lease and is typically priced around deployment, platform licensing, and support. WECORE Hub is for when you want to turn your capacity into revenue; its economics form around offering and billing that same capacity.

Infrastructure size (nodes, cores, memory), the number and type of GPUs, storage and network, and the deployment model and support level. Those are the axes that shape the estimate.

Where it fits your deployment model, usage can be metered hourly; but this page isn't about a retail tariff. A self-service, hourly public cloud is on the roadmap.
